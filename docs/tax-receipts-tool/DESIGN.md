## 1. Summary

We are building a single, GPO-owned tool that **replaces the entire tax-receipt
process** for the Green Party of Ontario. Today that process is a manual stack of
CiviCRM, hand-run SQL, Google Sheets, Apps Script, and Adobe. It is being retired
alongside CiviCRM itself as GPO migrates to **Qomon** (5-year contract signed
2026-04-02). Tax receipting and Elections Ontario reporting are explicit *gaps*
Qomon does not cover out of the box — GPO builds them in-house. **This tool is
that build.**

The tool:
- pulls **contributions** from Qomon (the new system of record) via the GPO
  **data warehouse** and the Qomon API,
- lets staff **view and edit contribution metadata** and **push changes back to
  Qomon**,
- **audits** contributions for compliance issues,
- **issues tax receipts** (PDF + sequential numbering) with an explicit
  contribution↔receipt allocation model that allows a contribution to back
  multiple receipts **without ever double-counting** a dollar,
- maintains an **immutable change-log / audit trail** (never delete — cancel
  only), and
- **generates the Elections Ontario reports** (per-entity AR-1 contribution
  rosters, S2P2) that the current Apps Script produces.

## 2. Goals & non-goals

**Goals**
- Replace 100% of the current manual tax-receipt workflow before the 2026 receipt
  cycle (run in early 2027, after migration).
- Make corrections (cancel / reissue / refund / move-between-donors) first-class,
  safe, and audited — these are the dominant source of today's pain.
- Guarantee the core invariant: **no contribution dollar is receipted twice**,
  even when a contribution is split across multiple receipts.
- Produce Elections-Ontario-valid outputs and pass the reconciliation checks the
  team performs by hand today.

**Non-goals**
- Not a payment processor. Money movement stays in Qomon/Stripe.
- Not a CiviCRM integration. CiviCRM is decommissioned at migration; we do **not**
  build against it. (See §4.)
- Not a general CRM. Contacts/transactions live in Qomon; we read/annotate, we do
  not own them.

## 4. Background

### 4.1 How it works today (being replaced)
- Contributions live in **CiviCRM** (`civicrm_contribution` + custom fields
  `civicrm_value_reporting_4`: reporting period, target entity, goods/services,
  EO date). CAs submit **Tax Receipt Requests (TRRs)** through a **Drupal tool**
  where they enter the contributions they received directly. _(Upstream source —
  see Open Question O7: repo link needed.)_
- Receipts are produced by **hand-run SQL** (`gpo-data/reports/tax-receipts/`):
  a trial batch table assigns sequential numbers (`GPO-00000001…`), de-dupes via
  a `cdntaxreceipts_log_contributions` junction table (`WHERE … IS NULL`),
  aggregates per `(contact, period, entity, category, goods/services)`, is
  reviewed in Google Sheets, then moved into CiviCRM.
- PDFs are stamped from a template (`pdf-lib`), 3 copies/page.
- Elections Ontario reports are generated by an **Apps Script** ("Generate EO
  Reports") bound to a master Google Sheet, split per entity.

### 4.2 Why it's being replaced
- GPO is replacing CiviCRM **and** GVote with **Qomon** + a GPO-owned **BigQuery**
  data warehouse. Migration CiviCRM→Qomon is scheduled for **late January 2027**,
  with a parallel-run safety net; year-end fundraising stays on old systems until
  cutover.
- The manual process is fragile: every correction forces a full-year
  cancel-and-re-enter of a donor; data is kept in many hand-synced artifacts
  (Civi, master receipt list, per-entity reports, S2P2 + Regular, Sheet **and**
  CSV); there is **no change-log**, so the team re-sends *all* entity reports
  because they can't tell which changed.

### 4.3 Hard external constraints (regulatory)
- **Regime is provincial — Elections Ontario, _not_ CRA.** Receipts are issued by
  registered Constituency Associations (CAs) / the party under the *Election
  Finances Act*.
- **AR-1** annual financial return + **all issued/cancelled/voided receipt copies**
  due to Elections Ontario by **May 31** of the following year. Audited statements
  if ≥ $10,000. **6-year** record retention.
- **Receipts must use EO pre-numbered forms or an EO-approved electronic
  database.** Receipt numbering must be **sequential and continuous** (reissues
  continue the sequence; e.g. `GPO-00321875 → …876`).
- Required receipt fields: acceptance date, issuance date, `$`-prefixed amount,
  form (monetary vs goods & services), full donor name + address, issuing CA,
  authorized signature (**e-signature allowed**).
- **2026 contribution limit: $3,425 per person**, aggregated **party-wide** across
  all CAs + nomination contestants (indexed yearly). No anonymous contributions
  (return or remit to EO). G&S aggregate ≤ $100/yr may be excluded at donor's
  option. GPO takes a **5% agency fee** on CA contributions transferred quarterly.
- **Voided receipts must be retained** (not destroyed) and submitted to EO;
  cancellations require a cancellation notice/form filed with EO.

## 5. Domain model

The central design problem is the **contribution ↔ receipt relationship**.
CiviCRM modelled it 1:1-ish via a junction row and prevented re-use. Our
requirement is richer:

> A contribution may be linked to **multiple** tax receipts, but **no
> contribution dollar may be counted twice** within issued receipts.

We model this with an explicit **allocation** entity.

```
Contact (Qomon person; mirrored read-only + local annotations)
  └─ Contribution (Qomon transaction: amount, date, type, status,
        payment method, campaign code, contact)
        ├─ ContributionMetadata (OUR data: reporting period, target entity,
        │     donation category [PARTY|ASSOC|CANDID|AGENCY], goods/services,
        │     eligibility flags, EO contributor id) — synced to Qomon when
        │     custom transaction fields exist (see Risk R1)
        └─ ReceiptAllocation (n) ──► Receipt
              amount_allocated  (Σ per contribution ≤ contribution.eligible_amount)

Receipt
  - receipt_number (sequential, continuous, never reused)
  - status (ISSUED | CANCELLED | VOID | REISSUED_FROM/TO)
  - issuing_entity (CA / GPO central), issue_date, acceptance_date
  - contact snapshot (name + address AT TIME OF PERIOD — see edge cases)
  - allocations[] (links to contributions + amounts)

ChangeLogEntry (append-only; never delete)
  - subject (contribution | receipt | allocation), before, after, actor, reason, ts
```

**Core invariants (enforced in DB + service layer):**
- `Σ allocation.amount_allocated` for a contribution ≤ its eligible amount
  (`total − non_deductible`). Prevents double-counting.
- A receipt's total = `Σ` of its allocations.
- Receipt numbers are allocated from a single monotonic sequence; cancel/void
  never frees a number.
- Nothing is hard-deleted. "Delete" = cancel/void with a change-log entry.

**Reporting groupings** (mirror EO requirements): by **target entity** × event
(Annual vs General Election), then donation category and goods/services.

## 6. Architecture

```
gpo-monolith/
  apps/
    tax-receipts/
      api/        Fastify + Zod (typed routes, schema-first), Prisma, PostgreSQL
      web/        Vite + React + Mantine + Tanstack Router/Query
  packages/
    tax-receipts-core/   domain types, Zod schemas, allocation/invariant logic
    qomon-client/        typed Qomon REST client (Bearer API key)
    warehouse-client/    BigQuery read client
```

- **System of record:** Qomon (transactions + contacts). **Reporting read layer:**
  the **BigQuery warehouse** — per GPO's plan, the receipt/EO tool reads donation
  data *from the warehouse*, not directly from Qomon, for reporting/aggregation.
- **Our own PostgreSQL** holds what Qomon/warehouse do not: receipts, receipt
  numbers, allocations, the immutable change-log, correction history, and
  (interim) contribution metadata until Qomon custom transaction fields land.
- **Sync model: polling-primary.** Qomon supports webhooks + polling and
  recommends polling as the resilient primary; new-donation webhooks appear
  bespoke/in-progress. Design a scheduled pull; treat webhooks as an optimization.
- **Auth:** Passport + bcrypt, stateful sessions (per the library review).
  Authorization at the **query layer** (CASL + Prisma) so per-entity (per-riding)
  data access is enforced centrally, not per-route.
- **Dead code:** the existing NestJS app under `src/` is slated for removal once
  this tool stands on its own (tracked separately; not blocking).

## 7. Phased roadmap

Each phase is independently useful and shippable.

### Phase 0 — Foundations
Scaffold `apps/tax-receipts` on the go-forward stack; CI (Vitest, ESLint, build);
auth + session; `qomon-client` (read transactions/contacts, API-key auth);
`warehouse-client` (BigQuery read); Prisma schema for contributions mirror +
metadata + change-log.

### Phase 1 — Contributions: view, edit metadata, push back to Qomon  ⟵ *first ship*
- Pull contributions (from warehouse for lists/aggregates; Qomon API for live
  detail/write).
- UI: searchable/filterable contributions list + detail view.
- Edit contribution **metadata** (reporting period, target entity, donation
  category, goods/services, eligibility).
- **Write back to Qomon.** ⚠️ Depends on Qomon **custom transaction fields**
  (committed H2 2026). **Interim fallback** until they exist: persist metadata in
  our PostgreSQL (+ optionally encode into Qomon campaign-code strings), and
  reconcile when the fields ship. This keeps Phase 1 unblocked.
- Every edit writes a change-log entry.

### Phase 2 — Audit / validation
Flag contributions with issues: donor over the **$3,425 party-wide annual limit**,
**donor not resident in Ontario** (ineligible / non-resident → must refund),
ineligible source, duplicate TRRs, contribution date outside reporting period,
missing/orphan target entity, agency-fee inconsistencies. Surface as a review
queue with resolution tracking.

### Phase 3 — Receipt issuance & corrections
- Generate receipts: sequential numbering, PDF (template stamp), required EO
  fields, e-signature.
- **Allocation model** enforcing no-double-count (a contribution may back multiple
  receipts).
- Correction workflows as first-class operations (today's biggest pain):
  **cancel**, **reissue** (continues the number sequence), **refund**, **move a
  whole receipt person→person**, **move some contributions person→person** — each
  generating cancellation forms and audit entries, never deleting.

### Phase 4 — Elections Ontario reporting (port "Generate EO Reports")
- Produce **per-entity AR-1 contribution rosters** split by target entity × event,
  in the EO upload column format (see §9), plus S2P2.
- Implement the reconciliation checks the macros perform today (R1–R8, §9) as
  automated validations — replacing the manual pivot-table QA.
- **Change-log-driven report diffs:** know exactly which entity reports changed so
  we stop re-sending all of them.

### Phase 5 — Cutover & full 2026 cycle
Align with the Jan–Feb 2027 CiviCRM→Qomon migration + parallel run. Run the full
2026 receipt cycle on the new tool. Validate against legacy outputs during the
parallel period.

## 8. Edge cases the tool must handle natively
From Slack history + the compliance issue tracker:
- **Ineligible / non-resident donor** (e.g. moved out of province): full refund of
  post-move contributions + cancel/reissue receipts; cannot amend prior-year
  returns → note in current return.
- **Duplicate TRRs → duplicate receipts** (CA submits twice): detect/flag.
- **Corrected amounts** (entered wrong): cancel + reissue + fix entity report.
- **Contributions split across receipts / spousal joint filing.**
- **Address-at-time-of-period:** donor with different addresses across annual vs
  election periods → receipt must use the correct period's address.
- **Returned-to-sender (RTS)** receipts surfacing wrong/missing records.
- **Vacant / unreachable CFO**, **NIL returns** (monthlies only, no cash/cheque),
  **placeholder / non-existent entities** (IDs `00001/00002`, "None"/"NIL").
- **Summary ↔ filed-return mismatches** (CR-1 queries), **agency-amount
  discrepancies**, **multiple open EO queries** on one return.

## 9. Reference: EO report output & validation rules

**Per-entity AR-1 roster columns** (target output format, confirmed from generated
reports):
`Party_ID, Contributor_ID, Receipt_Number, Receipt_Status, Agency_Contribution,
Political_Entity_Type, Political_Entity, Contribution_Amount, Contribution_Type,
Acceptance_Date, Receipt_Issuance_Date, Contribution_Period_ID, Contributor_Type,
Contributor_Last_Name, Contributor_First_Name, Organization_Name,
Contributor_Address, Contributor_City, Contributor_Province,
Contributor_Postal_Code`
(dates `MMDDYYYY`; e.g. `Receipt_Status=I`, `Agency_Contribution=Y`,
`Political_Entity_Type=A`, `Contribution_Type=MO`).

**Validation rules (replace the manual macros/pivots):**
- **R1** Σ(contribution amounts for a request) == receipt total.
- **R2** Σ(per-entity contributions) == entity's submitted Tax Receipt Summary ==
  filed return total.
- **R3** No contribution/request receipted twice.
- **R4** Every contribution maps to a valid target entity (no orphans).
- **R5** Agency-contribution flag consistent; 5% agency fee reconciles.
- **R6** Contribution date within its reporting period; Dec-paid-next-year flagged
  "receivable."
- **R7** Only issued receipts (`Receipt_Status=I`) exported; cancelled/void
  excluded but retained.
- **R8** Donor annual-limit aggregation across all receipts per donor.

> ⚠️ The authoritative 2025 master sheet, its Apps Script, and macros are
> DLP-locked from automated access; §9 is **inferred from surrounding artifacts**
> and must be confirmed against the live sheet by a human before Phase 4 build.

## 10. Working data model (input schema, confirmed)
Current TRR/EO export (row-per-contribution): `request_id,
tax_receipt_request_id, Contribution Date, Total Amount, Non-deductible Amount,
export_good_services (0 | "Goods & Services"), FirstName, LastName, StreetAddress,
City, Email, Contribution Receiver, Target Entity, Updated At, Created At`. This is
the practical shape of a contribution the new model must absorb from the Drupal TRR
tool (and, post-migration, from Qomon transactions).
